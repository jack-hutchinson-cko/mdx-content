<FAQItem title="How do I create new users on the Hub?" popularity={39}>

If you are the administrator of your Hub account, you can add new users from the <ExternalLink href="https://hub.checkout.com/login" >Hub</ExternalLink>. Go to _Settings > User management_ and click **New user**.

Once created, their profile will be added to the user list and they will receive an email inviting them to log in.

Take a look at our [Hub settings guide](/the-hub/update-your-hub-settings) for more information.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="How do you calculate the interchange (IC) fees?" popularity={19}>

We pay interchange fees to your issuing bank to cover the handling costs and inherent risks (such as bad credit and fraud) involved in approving payments.

These fees are influenced by various factors, including:

- **Transaction type** (domestic vs international; point-of-sale vs online)
- **Type of card** (credit, debit or pre-paid; commercial or consumer)
- **Industry** (merchants operating in higher-risk industries generally pay higher fees)

If you want to see the current interchange rate tables, contact your customer success manager and they can share them with you.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="What will happen to my funds after I terminate my account?" popularity={24}>

All funds (the settlement payouts and cumulative reserves) will be kept by <ExternalLink href="https://www.checkout.com" >Checkout.com</ExternalLink> until all reserves are released. The exact time the funds will be held is set out in your Merchant Services Agreement; it will be a number of weeks after the last transaction date.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="What will happen to my reserves after I terminate my account?" popularity={24}>

The cumulative reserves will be released gradually after the holding periods, as scheduled, and will be added to the settlement payouts.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="Will I receive the exact amount that was on my account when it was terminated?" popularity={25}>

Refunds, chargebacks, and associated fees will be deducted from the funds held, and the balance that is left after these deductions will be paid back to your account.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="I've terminated my account. When will the final payment be made?" popularity={26}>

Final payment will be made once all reserves have been released. The exact time this will take is set out in your Merchant Services Agreement; it will be a number of weeks after the last transaction date.

</FAQItem>

<FAQItem title="What should I do if I want to change my bank account to receive the final payment?" popularity={26}>

As per Checkout.com's policies, settlement is strictly made to the bank account as stated in the Merchant Services Agreement. You should, therefore, keep your bank account operational to allow for final settlement.

</FAQItem>
