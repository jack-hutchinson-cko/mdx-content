The table below explains the card verification value (CVV) response codes that you might receive when you are carrying out a CVV check or [verifying a card](/risk-management/card-verification).

<Table>
<TableHead headers={['CVV code', 'Description']} />

<TableBody>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

X

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

No CVV2 information is available

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

U

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

The issuer has not certified or has not provided the encryption keys to
the interchange

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

P

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

Card verification not performed, CVD was not on the card. Not all cards
have a CVD value encoded

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

Y

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

Card verification performed, and CVD was valid

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

D

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

Card verification performed, and CVD was invalid

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
<TableRow>
<TableCell>

N

</TableCell>
<TableCell>

Authorizing entity has not attempted card verification or could not
verify the CVD due to a security device error

</TableCell>
</TableRow>
</TableBody>
</Table>
